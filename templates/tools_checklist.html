🟡 1. Speed to Lead (Sales)

  • Lead into the CRM → call within 15 minutes. Voicemail + text if no answer.
  • Log the contact attempt immediately, so the board clock is accurate.
  • On the call: address, roof type, problem, decision-maker, insurance vs. retail.
  • Book the inspection before you hang up — pick the date/time and log it.
  • Karla: once the lead is entered, create the RoofGraf + measurements and upload it into the job documents — same day.
  • Estimate from the correct template, delivered within 48 hrs of inspection.
  • 2 follow-ups if no answer (day 2, day 4), then it's a cold prospect — not dead.

🟠 2. Job Turnover — Signed → Building

  • Scan ALL signed docs into the job folder — every page, legible.
  • Email copies of the signed docs to the client the same day — they get their paperwork in writing.
  • Collect the deposit — a job isn't really "approved" on the board until the deposit is in.
  • Permit packet started same day.
  • Material take-off started same day.
  • HOA path checked same day — if HOA, start that packet before/alongside the permit.
  • Job moved to the right stage + status note on the board; Production knows the start window.

🔴 3. Permits — Hiccup #1 (Build Permit Packet)

  • Confirm AHJ from the address (PBC auto-fills; Broward = HVHZ forms).
  • Confirm roofing system AND underlayment before building — wrong system = reject. (Tile can be single- or 2-ply — verify.)
  • NOC filled completely: owner, contractor, PCN/folio, legal description. No blanks.
  • Wind-load / product-approval sheets attached for the system.
  • PE seal on wind calcs where required — don't submit without it.
  • Packet saved + submitted; log the submittal date on the board.
Top rejection causes: wrong system/deck pairing, incomplete NOC, missing PCN/legal, missing product approval, no PE seal.

🟣 4. HOA Approval — Hiccup #2 (Office)

  • Submit color/material/sample package before or alongside the permit — not after.
  • Find the board/ARC meeting date — that's your real deadline. Work backward.
  • Follow up every 5 business days. Call the property manager.
  • Get approval in writing and save to the file before scheduling the build.
  • If HOA is the only blocker, flag it on the board.
  • Never start a build in an HOA community without written ARC approval in the file.

📦 5. Material Ordering — Hiccup #3 (Ops)

  • Take-off built from the RoofGraf measurements — squares, ridge, valley, accessories. No guessing.
  • Order placed same day the permit is submitted — do NOT wait for approval.
  • Confirm supplier lead time in writing; stage delivery 1–2 days before build start.
  • Confirm delivery + dumpster access with customer/HOA (drop location, gate).
  • Verify color/profile matches the HOA-approved sample before the truck rolls.
  • Material on site + permit box + yard sign staged before the crew shows up.

✅ Job Closeout (every job)

  • Final inspection passed and logged; final 10% collected.
  • Permit box removed; yard sign installed for the agreed period.
  • Ask for 3 referrals (names + numbers) — write them on the call sheet.
  • Ask for the Google review while the customer is happy.
  • Warranty docs delivered; job moved to Closed.

📅 Daily 10-Minute Standup

  • Any new lead older than 1 hour with no contact? → Sales fixes now.
  • Any job past its stage clock (permit/HOA/material)? → name the blocker + owner.
  • What's building this week — is material + permit + HOA confirmed for each?
One owner per blocker. No "we're waiting on them" without a follow-up date attached.
